War is a simple and straightforward game that can be played with just a deck of cards and two players. View wiki

Rules

  1. The game is played with a standard deck of 52 playing cards (No Jokers)
  2. The deck is shuffled and then divided evenly among the players
  3. Each player turns over the top card from their pile of cards at the same time
  4. The player with the higher card wins that round and takes both cards, placing them face down at the bottom of their pile
  5. If the two cards turned over are of the same rank (e.g., both players turn over a 6), then a "war" is declared. Each player places three cards face down and then turns over a fourth card. The player with the highest fourth card wins all the cards in that round. If the fourth cards are also tied, then the process repeats until there is a winner
  6. The game continues until one player has won all of the cards
  7. Have fun!

History:

The origins of the game War are not clear, but it is believed to have originated in the United States in the 19th century. The game became popular among soldiers during World War I and World War II, as it was a simple game that could be played with just a deck of cards. The game has since become a classic family game that is enjoyed by people of all ages.

Gameplay:

The game of War is a simple game of chance that requires no special skills or strategies. Each player starts with an equal number of cards and the goal of the game is to win all the cards by being the first player to collect all the cards in the deck.
